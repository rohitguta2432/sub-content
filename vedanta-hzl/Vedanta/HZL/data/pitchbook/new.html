GLOBAL Economics Trends

Overview of Global Economy
  • Given higher uncertainty and less effective stimulus packages, the global economic recovery is mainly driven by consumption.
  • Besides Brexit, European political uncertianty is a concern.
  • Debt problems and slow growth environment give both EU and China downside risks.

United States
  • GDP is expected to grow by 1.5% in 2016 supported by consumer spending and is projected to grow by 2% in 2017.
  • One rate hike expected in 2016 due to strong labor market, low unemployment and gradual rise in inflation outlook.
  • USD strength to resume as market expects a policy rate hike.

Europe
  • GDP is expected to expand by 1.6% in 2016 driven by consumer spending and is likely to stand at 1.4% in 2017.
  • Weak economy and low inflation could cause ECB to ease further by extending QE program beyond March 2017.
  • EUR in depreciating trend against USD after Brexit.

Japan
  • GDP for 2016 is 0.5% mainly supported by government spending and is likely to be in a range of 0.5-1% in 2017.
  • Massive fiscal stimulus package of JPY 28 trillion announced.
  • JPY is likely to depreciate against USD as a result of Fed's rate hike and additional monetary easing from BOJ.

China
  • Forecasted GDP in 2016 is 6.6% driven by domestic consumption, but is projected to slow to 6.2% in 2017.
  • Further policy easing is expected due to slowdown in 2H2016.
  • CNY depreciation from ongoing capital outflows.

ASEAN (CLMV)
  • Cambodia's GDP is forecasted to grow by 7% in 2016 due to solid construction and garment manufacturing sectors.
  • Laos' GDP growth is likely to expand at a reasonable pace of 7.4% in 2016 thanks mainly to a booming hydropower sector.
  • Myanmar's GDP is expected to grow by 8.6% in 2016. Further details on new economic policy remain to be seen.
  • Vietnam's GDP would grow at an average of 6.3% in 2016 thanks to firm export growth, strong labor market and FDI inflows.

Commodity (Oil)
  • By the end of 2016, oil price is expected to average at 44 USD/barrel and hover around 52 USD/barrel in 2017.
  • Bull factor: oil consumption increases during the winter (4Q16) and production freeze by Iran.
  • Bear factor: overall excess oil supply

Special Issue: US Election
  • Poll results and policy uncertainties remain high in 4Q2016.
  • Trump's policies are likely to create volatility both in the real sector and financial markets from protectionism and Americanism policies.
  • Clinton's tax policies create risks both on investment and trade by increasing tax rates.
